Our Mission

#WeLoveCinema

We expose all criminal content in fictional movies published in the mainland China that have not been legally disposed of according to mainland laws, for the smooth progress of censorship and release of every film we love.

Our Motto

#LiveByJustice

Justice has long arms, while our fictional justice has ULTRAlong ones.

Our Targets

#ZeroTolerance

Fictional Fugitive Criminals: in mainland movies, novels or any other mainland fictional creations.

Punish Fictional Criminals

Fictional Crime Investigation is an amateur online campaign founded by numbers of anonymous movie lovers on their own initiative. It bears the aspirations of most movie lovers in the mainland: they desire to watch movies from home and abroad in the mainland as early and ASAP.

However, due to the fictionality of the content of movies and novels, many villainous characters in fictional works are obeying and getting away with mainland laws and regulations in the virtual world, jeopardizing social justice and equity. This leads to movies not being shelved on the mainland in a timely manner. Therefore, we hope to pool our wisdom and gather the power of movie fans at home and abroad to report these characters to the censors to arrest them in the virtual world, which allows mainlanders to see more movies and novels, and brings more creators to a substantial profit in the mainland market and improve their life conditions.

With the influence of our members, so far many virtual characters have been punished in their respective fictional worlds due to reports from our anonymous members or self-censorship declarations by authors. This results in the timely release of the related works on the mainland, having avoided unnecessary financial losses. Yes, although reporting virtual criminals will not bring you hundreds of thousands of RMB bounty like reporting actual criminals, we believe that your justice and help to artists is priceless. Remember, you are to be our cinematic hero/heroine.

 

Famous Reported Cases

Li Weijie

Sheep Without A Shepherd

Li comes from a movie adapted from an Indian movie "Drishyam". Unlike the correponding character in the original version who concealed the truth, he turned himself in encourage by the filmmaker, because he needed to take his duty as a father and he was doomed to his fate.

Cheng Yong

Dying to Survive

Cheng Yong is a civic hero because he smuggled tablets for poor cancer patients that were very expensive in China, but he was arrested as a smuggler. After that, the government had included the tablets into health care insurance that much lowered the price.

Shui Qing & Qu Ting

The Oldtown Girls

The mother and daughter had kidnapped a classmate of the daughter and killed the poor girl by misadventure. They wanted to run, but that impossible in the movie of a rule of law society.

Jack

Fight Club

Jack was reported by our community member, Mr. Tyler Durden encourage by the company of Tencent Video who owns Fight Club's copyright in mainland. They persuade Mr. Durden to call the police, promised that if Jack was arrested, they would earn much by successfully streaming the movie. However, since the film was in 1999 (out of prosecution period), their trick had failed and Jack was tolerated to do evil.

Our Communnity

We support all film lovers who wish to join us to form a community that defend the justice of mainland film content. We encourage everyone to participate in the discussion as one to help more films, books and other creations to eliminate the injustice in their fictional worlds and help villains clean up their act, so that more books and films can be published and gain revenue in mainland China, the world's largest consumer market. Since overseas audiences are able to watch movies that have not yet been imported and censored in mainland China, we encourage overseas audiences in reporting content that violates the mainland’s law and helping imported movies in their censoring and editing. Remember, we do all for our beloved movies.
